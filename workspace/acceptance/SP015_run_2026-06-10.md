## Fixes landed (resolution / canonicalization only — both tested)

- **#1 roster-first disambiguation** (`PostgresRepository.resolve_entity`): on an ambiguous
  bare name, restrict to `seeded` candidates and return the unique one — undoing the
  `metric|HelixPay` dupe (minted when the extractor guessed the type) that made the grader's
  no-type lookup return `None`. Name-traps preserved (two-Marias/two-Tans are *two seeded*
  people → stay ambiguous). Test `test_resolve_entity_prefers_unique_seeded_over_minted_dupe`.
- **#2 period-qualified predicate canonicalization** (`canonical_predicate`/`canonical_key`):
  strip a leading quarter/half/FY/year qualifier before the alias lookup so `"Q1 2026 revenue"`
  → `revenue`; a distinct suffix (`"revenue vs plan"`) stays its own predicate. Test
  `test_period_qualifier_is_stripped_before_canonicalizing`.

| CRM cutover | `CRM migration, completion_target, end June` | `HubSpot migration (Brasil), "committed cutover date"` | surface-form split + predicate |
| core top contributor | `helixpay/core, top_contributor, Sara` (claim) | `Sara —owns→ helixpay/core` (a **link**) | claim-vs-link modeling |
| açaí owner | `Maria Santos —owns→ Açaí Express SP` | link absent (endpoint unresolved at ingest) | dropped link |

**Conclusion:** the dominant blocker is **extraction attribution** — metric-card values are
modeled as *metric entities* (`Revenue`, `Aggregate NPS`) rather than as claims on the
company; resolution cannot consolidate them without corrupting the metric ontology. Reaching
the **≥85% bar requires extraction-side changes** (a prompt change to attribute metric cards
to the document's company + emit canonical predicates) and a **paid re-record** — not further
resolution tweaks. **Fix #3 is deferred** pending that operator decision.
