# SP_021: Lift the image scope-cut — structured chart extraction + recall-bar golden

## Sprint Goal

Replace the **caption-only** image scope-cut (documented in `image.py:5-6`, golden
`image-revenue-trend-caption` carries `recall_bar: false`) with **structured per-series chart
extraction**, and prove it with **`recall_bar: true` golden facts sourced to the image** so the
$0 grader prints each chart datapoint FOUND/MISSING. Backend stays the **frozen Sonnet vision
model** (operator decision) — no vLLM/GPU, no deviation from `extraction = claude-sonnet-4-6`.

## Why this is now gradeable (the source-match insight)

`eval/run.py:_check_claim_fact` (139-144) requires the satisfying claim to carry the golden
`source_uri` (right value + **wrong/absent source** ⇒ `mismatch`, not `found`). So a golden fact
sourced to `data/images/revenue-trend-q1-2026.jpeg` can **only** be satisfied by a claim that
actually carries the **image** as its evidence — the same 4.8M value present in the interview
text won't satisfy it. **An image-sourced recall-bar fact is therefore a genuine proof that the
image was extracted**, which is exactly the operator's "see that it was extracted" requirement.

## Ground truth (text-corroborated — not eyeballed pixels)

The chart is a line chart, "HelixPay — Revenue by region (SGD M)", source line "FP&A · Q1 2026
close" (⇒ as_of `2026-03-31`), quarters Q1'25→Q1'26, four series (SEA actual, Brasil actual (SGD
eq), SEA plan, Brasil plan). The two **latest-quarter actuals** are independently corroborated by
corpus text, which is what makes them safe recall-bar facts:
- **Brasil actual Q1'26 ≈ SGD 4.8M** — matches existing golden `interview-brasil-revenue`
  (`HelixPay Brasil`/`revenue`/`SGD 4.8M`/`2026-03-31`, from `maria-silva.md`). The chart point
  sits cleanly at ~4.8.
- **SEA actual Q1'26 ≈ SGD 9.4M** — `all-hands-2026-04-15.md`: "Q1 closed at SGD 14.2M" total;
  14.2 − 4.8 (Brasil) ⇒ ~9.4 SEA. Chart point ~9.35–9.4.

Earlier-quarter points and the dashed **plan** lines are **not** graded (still approximate /
forward-looking) — we grade only the two text-anchored latest-quarter actuals.

## Current State

- `image.py` runs a **caption-level** vision pass (`_CAPTION_PROMPT`, 37-42) → prose caption →
  chunk → `extract_claims.md`. The caption already asks to "transcribe every visible number", but
  the output is unstructured prose and the extractor has **no chart guidance**, so per-series
  regional revenue claims are not reliably emitted and none are graded.
- Golden: only `image-revenue-trend-caption` (`recall_bar: false`) — caption, not datapoints.
- `HelixPay Brasil` is seeded (`roster.py:237`); `HelixPay SEA` is **not** (must be minted).

## Desired End State

- The image vision pass emits a **structured** transcription: per series (actual vs plan/target,
  solid vs dashed) and per period, with units (SGD M), the source as-of, and quarter labels mapped
  to quarter-end ISO dates.
- `extract_claims.md` has a **generic** "Charts & figures" section: a metric-by-region series ⇒
  one claim per region per period for **actual** series (`subject = HelixPay <Region>`,
  `predicate = revenue`, value w/ units, `as_of = period end`); **plan/target/dashed** series ⇒
  `hypothetical: true` (not competing actuals). No specific numbers in the prompt (no oracle-gaming).
- Two image-sourced `recall_bar: true` golden facts (SEA + Brasil Q1'26 revenue) grade FOUND
  after a paid image re-extraction; the existing caption fact stays `recall_bar: false`.

## Scope

In: sharpen `_CAPTION_PROMPT` (structured transcription); add the generic chart-extraction section

## Technical Approach

1. **`_CAPTION_PROMPT`** — keep generic, raise fidelity: instruct a structured transcription —
   title, units, source/as-of line; for **each** series, its value at **each** period; explicitly
   **distinguish actual vs plan/target** (solid vs dashed legend) and flag plan/target as
   forward-looking; map axis quarter labels ("Q1 26") to the quarter-**end** ISO date. Numbers come
   from the model reading the image — **no values baked into the prompt**.
2. **`extract_claims.md`** — add a short generic "Charts & figures (image captions)" subsection
   under the attribution examples: metric-by-region/segment series ⇒ one **actual** claim per
   region per period; `subject = "HelixPay <Region>"` (canonicalize "SEA"/"Southeast Asia" ⇒
   `HelixPay SEA`, "Brasil"/"Brazil" ⇒ `HelixPay Brasil`); `predicate` = the metric; value exactly
   as transcribed incl. units; `as_of` = the period end; **dashed/plan/target series ⇒
   `hypothetical: true`**. Describe the **shape**, never the corpus's numbers.
3. **Golden — edit the MASTER oracle, then REGENERATE** (Stage-3 process fix). `eval/smoke/facts.yaml`
   is **auto-generated** (banner: "do not hand-edit — re-run the builder") from
   `test/golden/facts.yaml` (the `DEFAULT_GOLDEN`, `run.py:54`). So: add to `test/golden/facts.yaml`
   near the existing image block (`:250-261`), then run `python -m eval.smoke.build_smoke` to
   regenerate the smoke subset verbatim. The image (`data/images/revenue-trend-q1-2026.jpeg`) **is**
   in the smoke manifest (`manifest.py:34`, "the image fidelity-loss path"), so the builder keeps
   the new facts. **Sample is out of scope (Stage-3 it.2 HIGH-1):** the image is **not** in
   `eval/sample/build_sample.py`'s manifest, so the sample subset would silently drop these facts —
   do not touch `eval/sample/facts.yaml`. Add:
   - `image-brasil-revenue-q1-2026`: `HelixPay Brasil`/`revenue`/`SGD 4.8M`/`2026-03-31`/image/`recall_bar: true`
     — the **gating anchor** (matches the independent text golden `interview-brasil-revenue`; chart point on a clean 4.8).
   - `image-sea-revenue-q1-2026`: `HelixPay SEA`/`revenue`/`SGD 9.4M`/`2026-03-31`/image/`recall_bar: true`
     — the **fidelity probe** (corroborated 14.2−4.8; a sub-gridline eyeball, so its verdict is
     reported as the honest fidelity signal — a miss is informative, not re-rigged). NB
     (Stage-3 it.2 MEDIUM-1): `normalize_value` keeps trailing zeros, so a `9.40` read won't
     substring-match `9.4m` — a `9.40` MISS is a normalization blind spot, not a chart-reading
     failure; record it as such in acceptance if it occurs.
   Keep `image-revenue-trend-caption` (`recall_bar: false`); retag its comment from "scope cut" to
   "caption corroboration (datapoints now graded by the two facts above)".
4. **Relax the golden guard test (Stage-3 it.2 CRIT-1).** `test/golden/test_golden.py:64-66`
   (`test_image_facts_are_informational_only`) asserts **every** `format=="image"` fact is
   `recall_bar is False` — written to enforce the now-lifted scope cut, so it fails the instant the
   two facts land. Rewrite it to assert the **caption** fact (`image-revenue-trend-caption`) stays
   `recall_bar: false` while permitting structured-datapoint image facts to be `recall_bar: true`
   (intent preserved: no silent return to caption-only bar grading). Update the stale header comment
   in `test/golden/facts.yaml` (~`:23`, "Image facts are caption-level only … recall_bar:false").

## Risks & Mitigations

- *Line-chart reads are approximate; SEA 9.4 vs a 9.3 read fails the substring grader* → grade only
  the two **text-corroborated** latest-quarter actuals; Brasil 4.8M is the rock-solid anchor (point
  on a clean value, matches an independent text golden), SEA 9.4M is corroborated by 14.2−4.8. If
  the paid read still misses SEA, that is an **honest** fidelity signal — report it, do not re-rig
  the prompt with the answer.
- *Oracle-gaming the prompt* → both prompts describe only the **shape** (series→claim mapping,
  actual-vs-plan), never the corpus numbers; the model must read values from the pixels.
- *`HelixPay SEA` mis-resolves / mints inconsistently* → canonicalize the regional subject in the
  extract guidance ("SEA"⇒`HelixPay SEA`); mint-only (no seed) keeps it distinct from `HelixPay`
  and `HelixPay Brasil` (roster.py:234 invariant: never alias regions onto the company).
- *Plan/target lines pollute actuals* → `hypothetical: true` on dashed/plan/target series so they
  are not stored as competing values (consistent with the existing `hypothetical` rule).
- *Re-running vision is non-deterministic* → consistent with the "extract locally, validate the
  snapshot, pg_dump to prod" promotion plan; we ship the validated bytes, not a re-run.

`image-brasil-revenue-q1-2026` and the existing `interview-brasil-revenue` share
subject/predicate/value/as_of and differ only in source. Supersession fires **same-source only**
(`pipeline.py:283`, `assemble.should_supersede`), so the two coexist (correct — never collapse
provenance). Identical values are not a contradiction, so `contradict.detect` adds none. The paid
acceptance asserts **both** Brasil claims persist (interview + image) and no spurious contradiction
appears on (`HelixPay Brasil`, `revenue`).

## HelixPay SEA: mint-only, not seeded (Stage-3 rec #4 — considered, declined)

The architect recommended seeding `HelixPay SEA` (mirroring the seeded `HelixPay Brasil`) for
robuster resolution. **Declined for SP_021, by design:** seeding touches `roster.py` (SP_020) and
`test/unit/seed/**` (SP_001) — a collision and a substrate expansion beyond this sprint's
resolution-light scope. Crucially, the **gating anchor (Brasil) is already seeded and robust**; SEA
is the stretch probe, so mint-only risk falls on the non-gating fact. Mitigation: the extract
guidance canonicalizes the regional subject to `HelixPay SEA`, and the paid acceptance asserts
**exactly one** `HelixPay SEA` row exists (not a surface-form split). If the paid read shows SEA
resolution fragility, seeding it is the documented follow-up.
